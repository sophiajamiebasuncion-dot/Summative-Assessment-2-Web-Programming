Outer Planets

The outer planets are beyond the asteroid belt and are considered gas and ice giants. They are the following:

  1. Jupiter
  2. Saturn
  3. Uranus
  4. Neptune
    5.

These planets are much larger and mostly composed of hydrogen, helium, and ices like water, methane, and ammonia. They also have extensive systems of rings and moons.

Planet Nine

Mystery planet!

In 2016, researchers proposed the possible existence of a ninth planet, for now, dubbed "Planet Nine" or Planet X. Scientists have not seen Planet Nine. They inferred its existence by its gravitational effects on other objects in the Kuiper Belt, a region at the fringe of the solar system that is home to icy rocks left over from the birth of the solar system.
