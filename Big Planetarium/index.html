On Our Way to the Milky Way!

The Sun, along with all the planets that orbit it, belongs to a galaxy called the Milky Way. A galaxy is a vast collection of stars, gas, and dust that are held together by gravity. The Milky Way is classified as a barred spiral galaxy and is estimated to be about 13.6 billion years old.

  1. Galaxy type: Barred spiral
  2. Age: 13.6 billion years (and counting)
  3. Diameter: 100,000 light-years
  4. Size: 100,000 light-years across
  5. Number of stars: 400 billion
  6. Rotation time: 250 million years

While the Milky Way is unimaginably vast, our Solar System is tucked away in a smaller, quieter neighborhood known as the Orion Arm. We are located about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center—roughly halfway between the center and the edge of the disk. At the very heart of the Milky Way lies Sagittarius A*, a supermassive black hole with a mass equivalent to four million suns. Its immense gravitational pull acts as an anchor, keeping the hundreds of billions of stars in our galaxy locked in their grand, swirling dance. Despite traveling at a staggering speed of 515,000 miles per hour, it still takes our Sun millions of years to complete just one single lap around this cosmic center.

Our Solar System

Our solar system can be described as our local region in space, consisting of the Sun and everything that revolves around it. This includes planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and other small particles of dust and gas.

Furthermore

The planets are arranged in a specific order based on their distance from the Sun. Starting from the closest, they are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune and then the possible Planet Nine.
